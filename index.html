Discover Keith Fritz

My name is Keith Fritz, I am a dynamic robotics engineer based in Austin, Texas. With a solid foundation in mechatronics engineering and a keen interest in both the hardware and software facets of robotics, I navigate the cutting edge of technology. From programming robotic platforms using languages like ROS, C++, and Python to crafting intricate designs in CAD, I bring ideas to life. My experience involves everything from working with renowned robotic systems like Clearpath and Boston Dynamics to crafting custom solutions to suit very specific needs.

Within the Texas Robotics Consortium at the Univeristy of Texas at Austin, I have facilitated research for service and social robots, manipulators, autonomous vehicles, and human-robot interfaces through my hardware and software expertise on the robotics research platforms. I have been working in the design, prototyping, and integration of robotics for over two years. In this time, I have worked on a wide range of commercial robotics like encompassing renowned commercial models like Boston Dynamics Spot, Clearpath Husky and Jackal, Segway RMP220, Kinova Gen3, Universal Robots UR5, as well as numerous custom-built robots.
